Colour the Night

About

Through eye-catching and compelling visual design, I want to colour the night with my style. Utilizing a combination of both my engineering experience and love of graphic design, I am able to offer my unique perspective when it comes to problem solving using visual communication. As a fan of Tokyo's nightscapes and attractions, it is one of my goals to emulate that atmosphere and experience, to someday play a part in shaping Vancouver's own nightscape.

Before starting my career as a graphic designer, I was aspiring to become a mechanical engineer. Even though I chose to become a graphic designer instead, my years as a STEM student laid the groundwork for my way of thinking when it comes to the design process. When it comes to problem solving, the approach for both graphic designers and engineers are largely the same. Identifying the problem, assessing available resources and time, creating a structured plan, prototyping and revising, this process can be adapted for both engineers and graphic designers. This is the process I use when handling my projects.

I have always been a fan of Japanese culture and was fortunate enough to have been able to travel there. Traveling through various city centres and viewing different landmarks was a very refreshing experience. However, depending on the time of day, the experience changes. As the night starts, the atmosphere changes. It felt more interesting with all the neon lights and various signs that decorate Tokyo. It was as if you'd travelled to a different country altogether.

A close passion and hobby of mine is sketching. Influenced by a lot of Japanese media at a young age, this passion of mine built the core foundation of my abilities and style. I've used artbooks and albums as reference for my works, notably the illustrators Ayame Kojima of the Castlevania series, and Yoji Shinkawa of the Metal Gear series. Kojima evokes a lot of emotion with her pieces of work that I could only hope to emulate. Shinkawa's work uses a lot of basic, fundamental shapes, when combined they create a breathtaking illustration.
